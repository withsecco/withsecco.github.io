    Why psycholinguistics/ neurolinguistics?

    Language underpins how we think and talk. It's the tool we use to communicate thoughts. I'm particularly interested in how language is produced, comprehended, and coordinated in real-time interactions, specificlly,

    - How do interlocutors achieve mutual alignment in natural conversations, and how is turn-taking organized?
    - How do individuals integrate prior information to predict incoming content?
    - What information do individuals attend to while listening, and what is lost or used in subsequent turns?

    With the development of Artificial Intelligence (AI), I'm also interested in efforts to compare brain-like models with the human brain, e.g., studying the human brain to build better models, and/or using models to understand the human brain. I'm curious about how machines might one day debate with humans, persuade and convince us (changing our attitudes and beliefs), and emotionally connect with us.

    I'm combining linguistics, psychology, and neuroscience to explore the answers to these questions. Just as a language is a way of thinking, different disciplines attempt to tackle the same phenomenon from different perspectives. More dialogue is needed between these groups of researchers. Hope to cross paths in the furture!
